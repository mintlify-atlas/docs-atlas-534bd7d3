# Battery Systems

MotionTech vehicles are powered by advanced LifePO4 (Lithium Iron Phosphate) battery technology, offering superior safety, longevity, and performance compared to traditional battery chemistries. Our battery systems are specifically engineered to meet the demanding requirements of electric mobility.

## LifePO4 Technology Overview

LifePO4 represents the latest advancement in lithium battery chemistry, providing significant advantages for electric vehicle applications.

<Note>
LifePO4 batteries are inherently safer than other lithium-ion chemistries due to their stable chemical structure, which significantly reduces the risk of thermal runaway and fire.
</Note>

## Technical Characteristics

### Chemistry and Construction

- **Cathode Material**: Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4)
- **Cell Structure**: Prismatic or cylindrical cells (configuration dependent)
- **Battery Management**: Integrated BMS (Battery Management System)
- **Protection**: Overcharge, over-discharge, short circuit, temperature
- **Enclosure**: Weather-resistant housing suitable for vehicle mounting

### Performance Parameters

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Cycle Life** | 2,000+ cycles @ 80% DoD |
| **Self-Discharge** | Less than 3% per month |
| **Operating Temperature** | Charge: 0°C to 45°C<br/>Discharge: -20°C to 60°C |
| **Storage Temperature** | -10°C to 35°C (optimal) |
| **Charge Efficiency** | >95% |
| **Energy Density** | 90-120 Wh/kg (typical) |

<Warning>
Never charge LifePO4 batteries below 0°C (32°F). Charging in freezing temperatures can cause permanent damage to the cells. Store batteries in a temperature-controlled environment when not in use.
</Warning>

## Battery Management System (BMS)

All MotionTech battery systems include an integrated BMS that provides:

### Protection Functions

- **Overcharge Protection**: Prevents cell voltage from exceeding safe limits
- **Over-discharge Protection**: Disconnects load before cells are damaged
- **Overcurrent Protection**: Limits current to safe operating range
- **Short Circuit Protection**: Immediate disconnect in fault conditions
- **Temperature Monitoring**: Shuts down in extreme temperature conditions
- **Cell Balancing**: Equalizes charge across all cells for maximum life

### Monitoring Capabilities

- Real-time voltage monitoring
- Current flow measurement
- Temperature sensing
- State of Charge (SoC) calculation
- State of Health (SoH) estimation
- Fault code logging

## Charging Recommendations

For optimal battery life and performance:

### Charging Best Practices

1. **Use Compatible Chargers**: Only use LifePO4-specific chargers matched to voltage
2. **Charge Temperature**: Charge only between 0°C and 45°C
3. **Avoid Full Depletion**: Recharge before battery fully depletes when possible
4. **Regular Charging**: Don't leave batteries discharged for extended periods
5. **Storage Charge**: Store at 50-60% charge for long-term storage
6. **Ventilation**: Charge in well-ventilated areas

### Charging Times (Approximate)

| Battery System | Standard Charge | Fast Charge* |
|----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 48V-12Ah | 3-4 hours | 2-3 hours |
| 48V-30Ah | 6-8 hours | 4-5 hours |
| 60V-30Ah | 6-8 hours | 4-5 hours |
| 60V-90Ah | 10-12 hours | 6-8 hours |

*Fast charging requires compatible fast charger and may reduce overall cycle life if used exclusively.

Detailed charging procedures are available in our [Charging Guide](/guides/charging).

<Tip>
For maximum battery lifespan, use standard charging whenever time permits. Reserve fast charging for situations where rapid turnaround is essential.
</Tip>

## Range Factors

Actual range depends on multiple variables:

### Factors Affecting Range

- **Load Weight**: Heavier loads reduce range significantly
- **Terrain**: Hills and inclines consume more energy
- **Speed**: Higher speeds decrease efficiency and range
- **Temperature**: Cold weather reduces battery capacity temporarily
- **Riding Style**: Aggressive acceleration/braking reduces range
- **Tire Pressure**: Under-inflated tires increase rolling resistance
- **Battery Age**: Capacity gradually decreases over battery lifetime
- **Accessory Use**: Lights and other accessories consume power

### Range Optimization Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Maintain Proper Tire Pressure">
    Keep tires inflated to manufacturer specifications. Under-inflated tires can reduce range by 10-15% due to increased rolling resistance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Smooth Acceleration">
    Gradual acceleration is more efficient than rapid starts. Aggressive riding can reduce range by up to 20%.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Optimize Load">
    Carry only necessary weight. Every additional 50 kg reduces range by approximately 5-8%.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Temperature Management">
    Store and charge batteries at moderate temperatures. Cold batteries can lose 20-30% capacity temporarily.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Regular Maintenance">
    Keep drive systems clean and lubricated. Mechanical resistance from poor maintenance reduces efficiency.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Battery Lifespan and Degradation

LifePO4 batteries maintain capacity better than other lithium chemistries:

### Expected Lifespan

- **Cycle Life**: 2,000+ full charge/discharge cycles
- **Calendar Life**: 5-7 years typical service life
- **Capacity Retention**: 80% capacity after 2,000 cycles
- **End of Life**: 70-80% of original capacity

### Maximizing Battery Life

1. Avoid extreme temperatures during operation and charging
2. Don't store fully charged for extended periods
3. Perform regular maintenance charging if storing
4. Use appropriate chargers with proper voltage/current settings
5. Avoid complete discharge when possible
6. Keep batteries clean and dry
7. Monitor for swelling, damage, or unusual behavior

## Safety Considerations

<Warning>
**Critical Safety Rules**

- Never disassemble battery packs
- Don't expose to water or extreme heat
- Discontinue use if damaged, swollen, or leaking
- Never short circuit terminals
- Keep away from flammable materials during charging
- Dispose of old batteries through proper recycling channels
</Warning>

### In Case of Emergency

- **Battery Fire**: Use appropriate fire extinguisher (Class D or ABC). DO NOT use water.
- **Damage**: Isolate damaged battery in non-flammable area, contact support
- **Swelling**: Stop use immediately, do not charge, contact support
- **Leakage**: Avoid contact, ventilate area, handle with protective equipment

Refer to our [Safety Guide](/guides/safety) for complete emergency procedures.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why LifePO4 instead of standard lithium-ion?">
    LifePO4 offers superior safety (no thermal runaway risk), longer cycle life (2,000+ vs 500-1,000 cycles), better thermal stability, and environmental advantages. While energy density is slightly lower, the safety and longevity benefits far outweigh this for vehicle applications.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I upgrade to a higher capacity battery?">
    In some cases, yes. Contact our technical team to discuss compatibility. The vehicle's motor controller, charger, and mounting must be compatible with the higher capacity system. Professional installation is required.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I know when to replace the battery?">
    Replace when capacity drops below 70-80% of original (significant range reduction), if the battery shows physical damage, swelling, or overheating, or if charging time increases dramatically. Most batteries last 5-7 years with proper care.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use a different charger?">
    Only use chargers specifically designed for LifePO4 chemistry and matched to your battery's voltage. Standard lithium-ion chargers use different charging profiles and can damage LifePO4 batteries or reduce their lifespan.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What temperature is too cold for operation?">
    LifePO4 batteries can discharge safely down to -20°C, though capacity is reduced. Never charge below 0°C. For best performance and longevity, operate and store between 10°C and 35°C when possible.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
